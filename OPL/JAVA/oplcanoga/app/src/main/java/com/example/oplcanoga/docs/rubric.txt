Computer’s Strategy

Whether to cover or uncover

Cover First: If possible, you should always cover your own squares first as this would help you win and makes it harder for the opponent to uncover them.



Uncover If Needed: If covering isn't possible, then uncover the opponent's squares. This would mess up their progress and increases your chances of winning.



Covering its squares

Cover More Squares: For example… if the dice sum is 6, covering 1, 2, &amp; 3 (3 squares) is better than just 6 (1 square). The more the merrier



Cover High Numbers First: Covering bigger numbers like 9, 8, or 7 is wise. This stops your opponent from scoring big.



Block the Opponent: If the opponent is close to uncovering all their squares, cover the ones they need the most.



Uncovering opponent’s squares

Like covering Strategy…



Uncover More Squares: If the dice sum is 6, uncovering 1, 2, &amp; 3 (3 squares) is better than just 6 (1 square). The more the merrier



Target High-Value Squares: Uncover bigger numbers like 9, 8, or 7 to increase the opponent’s score.



Disadvantage your Opponent: If the opponent is close to winning, uncover the squares that will slow them down.



Throwing one or two dice when 7-n covered

Roll One Die: If all numbers from 7 to n are already covered, roll one die to avoid a high roll that can’t be used (This keeps the turn going instead of wasting it)



Roll Two Dice: If any squares from 7 to n are still uncovered, roll two dice to get a higher sum (This increases the chances of covering or uncovering more squares)
